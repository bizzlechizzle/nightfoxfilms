## Response Time Best Practices

**Inquiry Response:** Within 1 hour (or ASAP)
- Couples book the first vendor who responds 50% of the time
- Use mobile email to respond fast, even if it's just "Got your email! Will send full details in 2 hours"

**Consultation Follow-Up:** Within 2 hours of call
- Strike while iron is hot
- They're comparing vendors immediately after calls

**Proposal:** Within 24 hours of consultation
- Any longer = they're already talking to someone else

## Pro Tips

1. **Use their name.** Personalize every email‚Äîno generic "Dear Bride" nonsense.

2. **Ask questions.** Get them talking about their wedding. The more they share, the more invested they become.

3. **Create urgency (authentically).** If you really are talking to another couple about their date, say so. If not, don't lie.

4. **Show personality.** Your emails should sound like you, not a corporation. Be warm, friendly, conversational.

5. **Make it easy to say yes.** Clear next steps, booking links, calendar embeds, payment options‚Äîremove all friction.

6. **Follow up.** Most couples don't book after the first email. Have a follow-up plan (see lead nurture sequence).

7. **Mobile-optimize.** 70% of email opens are on mobile. Keep emails short, scannable, with clear CTAs.

## Tracking Success

**Metrics to monitor:**
- Response time (aim for <1 hour)
- Response rate (how many inquiries respond to your first email?)
- Consultation booking rate (how many emails lead to calls?)
- Proposal-to-booking rate (how many proposals convert?)

**Goal:**
- 80%+ response rate to initial inquiry
- 60%+ consultation booking rate
- 50%+ proposal-to-booking rate

If you're hitting these numbers, your emails are working. If not, test new copy.
